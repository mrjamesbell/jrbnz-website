# Product

## Register

brand

> Note: The Signal admin at `/signal/` is a separate **product**-register surface — design serves workflow, not identity. When working on Signal, override to `register: product` for that task.

## Users

Arts and cultural peers: theatre makers, writers, photographers, directors, collaborators. People with a practiced aesthetic eye who will notice craft or its absence. They arrive with existing respect for the medium and want to encounter a serious practitioner, not a promotional brochure. They read carefully, linger on images, and are put off by anything that feels built for an algorithm.

## Product Purpose

A personal site for James Bell — theatre maker, writer, business manager, and photographer from Auckland, New Zealand. The site exists to present James's work and thinking to peers in the arts and cultural sector. Success is a visitor feeling they've encountered a genuine creative intelligence: someone with range, rigour, and a distinct point of view.

## Brand Personality

Bold. Editorial. Grounded.

The register of a film festival catalogue or a literary journal from the Southern Hemisphere — takes up space without apology, makes strong choices, and stays rooted in a specific place and practice. Warm without being soft. Dark without being brooding. The cinematic design signals a practitioner who understands that form is content.

## Anti-references

- **Generic dev portfolio** — GitHub-green, dark card grids, hero metric blocks, project showcase structure
- **Lifestyle blogger / soft** — pastel palette, rounded cards, Instagram-adjacent warmth, "authentic" cliché
- **Slick SaaS / startup** — gradient CTAs, feature grids, conversion-funnel energy, "join now" buttons
- **Over-designed agency site** — scroll-jacking, cursor effects, flashy for its own sake, design as performance

## Design Principles

1. **Let the work breathe.** Generous whitespace and slow pacing are the message. A crowded layout implies anxiety; a still one implies confidence.
2. **Typography is the craft signal.** For peers who read, the quality of a serif line and the precision of a heading hierarchy communicate before any word does.
3. **Grounded in place.** Auckland, New Zealand — the Southern Hemisphere, the light, the distance — should inflect the aesthetic, not as kitsch but as specificity. Generic internationalism is a dead end.
4. **Bigger is more considered, not less.** Type and images run large. This is both an accessibility principle (the site's author prefers scale) and an editorial one: scale confers weight.
5. **Restraint earns drama.** The cinematic dark canvas and warm gold accent work because everything else is stripped back. Each decorative choice is a debt; keep the ledger short.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA contrast minimum. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for all animations and transitions. Default to larger body type — the designer is visually impaired and the aesthetic principle aligns: bigger type reads as editorial authority, not accommodation. Never go smaller to appear "refined."
