Directing

Since 2004, I have directed 42 productions across Shakespeare, modern classics, contemporary drama, comedy and new work. The work falls into two overlapping strands: a long association with Shakespeare in the Park, and, through Foolish Wit Theatre, a growing body of intimate, actor-led productions drawn to families, power, and people under pressure.

Two connected strands

One strand of the work is public and recurring: Shakespeare in the Park, large ensembles, and a long relationship with the outdoor amphitheatre. The other is more personal: Foolish Wit productions that keep returning to families, power, intimacy, and people forced into close quarters with one another.

The balance has shifted over time, but both are still visible. The Shakespeare work has provided a long spine; the recent Foolish Wit productions have made the shape of my own taste clearer.

Shakespeare

18 productions over 17 years, with several plays revisited later from a different point in life: Hamlet, Much Ado About Nothing, Romeo and Juliet, and Measure for Measure.

Families, power, pressure

Outside Shakespeare, the pull is often towards tightly wound plays: Doubt, The Crucible, The Lion in Winter, A Doll's House, The Glass Menagerie.

Whole-production work

The award recognition has tended to come across the whole production rather than in a single lane: acting, ensemble, direction, stage management, lighting, sound, costume, set and properties. That feels right to me. Directing is partly about individual performances, but also about making all the parts speak the same language.
